Privacy Policy

Last updated: February 2026

1. Data We Collect

Torve stores data locally on your device, including watch history and progress, preferences, API keys you enter for third-party services, playlist URLs, and favorites. This data is stored on your device and is not sent to Torve servers by default.

2. Data Sent to Third Parties

When you use external integrations, Torve may send data to the services you configure.

  • TMDB: search queries and media IDs for metadata lookup
  • Trakt.tv: watch history and watchlist if sync is enabled
  • AI providers: search queries sent to the AI API you configure
  • Streaming optimization services: content identifiers and your API key for availability checks
  • Jellyfin or Plex: library and playback requests for your connected media server

3. Analytics and Crash Reporting

The Google Play version of Torve includes Firebase Analytics and Crashlytics for crash reports and anonymous usage information. The Amazon Appstore version does not include Firebase or analytics SDKs.

4. Data Sharing

Torve does not sell, share, or transfer your personal data to advertisers or data brokers.

5. Third-Party Services

Third-party services connected to Torve are governed by their own privacy policies. Review those policies before connecting your accounts.

6. Data Security

API keys and sensitive credentials are encrypted using the Android Keystore. Other local app data is stored in the app's private storage.

7. Data Deletion

You can remove app data by clearing storage in Android Settings or uninstalling the app. Individual data such as watch history, playlists, and API keys can also be managed inside the app.

8. Contact

For privacy-related questions or concerns, contact privacy@torve.app.
